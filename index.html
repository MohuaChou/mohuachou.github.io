Rui Zhou

M.Sc. Student in Electronic Information, University of Science and Technology of China
I am interested in pre-training, self-supervised learning, medical image analysis, multimodal deep learning, and audio generation. My recent work spans data-efficient representation learning for 3D ultrasound analysis, multimodal prediction on PET/CT, and controllable audio generation based on MMAudio pipelines.

Research Areas

Pre-training & Self-Supervised Learning. Masked image modeling, data-efficient pre-training, representation learning under scarce annotation, and hybrid architectures for 3D medical imaging.
Medical Image Analysis. 3D ultrasound analysis, PET/CT-based multimodal prediction, segmentation, and clinically meaningful deep learning systems.
Multimodal Learning. Cross-modal modeling across image, audio, and text, with a focus on robust alignment and representation interaction.
Audio Generation. Event-centric and controllable video-to-audio generation, especially MMAudio style systems and late-stage control mechanisms.

Selected Projects

Controllable Audio Generation with MMAudio / Omnifoley

Ongoing
Current research · audio generation · controllability
Exploring controllable video-to-audio generation, especially how text conditions can recover influence from visually dominant late-stage conditioning. Current directions include clip attenuation, clip gating, event-centric generation, and controllability-grounding tradeoffs.

Grouped Cyclic Shift Masked Image Modeling for 3D Ultrasound

Major Revision
Shenzhen Mindray collaboration · pre-training · 3D ultrasound
A data-efficient self-supervised learning framework for 3D ultrasound analysis. The work combines Grouped Cyclic Shift, sparse computation, and Mask-Guided Feature Reformation to improve masked image modeling under scarce-data settings.
Code

PSMA PET/CT-based Multimodal Prediction of Pelvic Lymph-node Metastases

2023–2024
Algorithm lead · multimodal prediction · nuclear medicine
Built a multimodal deep learning pipeline for preoperative prediction of pelvic lymph-node metastases in prostate cancer patients, using PET/CT modeling, transfer learning, and machine learning classification.
