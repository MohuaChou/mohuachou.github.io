Research Areas

I focus on representation learning and practical multimodal systems, with a strong interest in how models learn structure from limited data and how conditional control can be improved in generation.

Pre-training & Self-Supervised Learning

Data-efficient pre-training, masked image modeling, hybrid CNN-MLP architectures, and learning robust representations under limited annotation.

Medical Image Analysis

3D ultrasound, PET/CT, segmentation, prediction modeling, and clinically meaningful multimodal analysis for healthcare applications.

Multimodal Learning

Cross-modal representation learning, vision-audio-text interaction, and multimodal prediction pipelines for medical and generative tasks.

Audio Generation

Event-centric audio generation, late-stage condition control, and controllability-grounding tradeoffs in MMAudio / video-to-audio systems.

Controllable Audio Generation with MMAudio / Omnifoley

Current research · audio generation · multimodal control
Ongoing
I am currently exploring controllable video-to-audio generation, especially how text can regain control from visually dominant late-stage conditions. The recent direction focuses on late-stage clip attenuation / clip gating, event-centric generation, and better controllability-grounding tradeoffs in MMAudio-based pipelines.
Current Research

Grouped Cyclic Shift Masked Image Modeling for 3D Ultrasound

Shenzhen Mindray collaboration · pre-training · 3D ultrasound analysis
Major Revision
A data-efficient self-supervised learning framework for 3D ultrasound analysis. The work combines a hybrid CNN-MLP encoder with Grouped Cyclic Shift and Mask-Guided Feature Reformation to make masked image modeling more effective under scarce-data settings.
Code

PSMA PET/CT-based Multimodal Prediction of Pelvic Lymph-node Metastases

Algorithm lead · multimodal prediction · nuclear medicine
2023–2024
Built a multimodal deep learning pipeline for preoperative prediction of lymph-node metastases in prostate cancer patients. The project used 3D ResNet-based PET/CT modeling, transfer learning, and SVM classification, and later evolved into the published EJNMMI paper.
